How Often Should You Reseal Pavers?

Homeowners want a simple resealing timeline, but the honest answer depends on how the surface is performing. In Florida, sun exposure, rain, traffic, algae pressure, and the original prep all influence how long a sealed surface stays in its maintenance window. The best schedule is based on real wear signs, not just the calendar.

Sealing is not the end of maintenance. It is the start of a simpler, more protective maintenance phase. Seasonal Paver Care Tips for Florida Homeowners

Florida paver maintenance is easier when homeowners think seasonally. The concerns shift through the year: pollen and debris in spring, heavy rain and algae in summer, leaf staining in fall, and maintenance planning during the drier stretches. A seasonal rhythm helps you catch issues early instead of reacting once the surface already looks worn.

Do You Need to Re-Sand Pavers Every Year?

Most homeowners do not need a full re-sanding every single year, but many do need to monitor their joints more often than they expect. In Florida, rain, cleaning habits, traffic, drainage, and edging conditions all affect how quickly joint sand starts thinning out. The right answer is based on the condition of the joints, not the date on the calendar.
