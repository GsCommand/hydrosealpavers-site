Where DIY Usually Breaks Down

Preparation gets underestimated

Most homeowners focus on the sealer can. The bigger variable is preparation: removing contamination, correcting joints, and getting the surface truly ready. Skipping any of those steps makes product performance irrelevant.

Sand replacement is often incomplete

Many DIY jobs add fresh sand on top of old material because full removal is time-intensive. That shortcut is one of the most common causes of rapid joint washout and uneven stabilization.

Moisture risk is harder than it looks

A driveway can look dry but still hold pore moisture from overnight humidity, irrigation, or shaded retention. Florida paver sealing succeeds when the surface is condition-ready, not when the calendar is open.

What Most DIY Jobs Miss

  • Old joint sand removal before repacking.
  • ASTM C144 selection for stable joint performance.
  • Controlled chemistry cleaning instead of pressure-only cleaning.
  • Product matching by surface type instead of one-sealer-for-everything logic.
  • Condition-based go/no-go decisions tied to weather and cure window.
