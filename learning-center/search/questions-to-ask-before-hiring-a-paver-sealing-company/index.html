Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Paver Sealing Company

If you want long-lasting results, ask detailed process questions first and use price only after you confirm exactly what each company is including.

Learning Center HomeBack to Search

TL;DR / Key Takeaways

  • The best questions to ask before hiring a paver sealing company are process questions, not branding questions.
  • If a contractor cannot explain prep clearly, the low quote is usually a shortcut quote.
  • Old sand removal, ASTM C144 repacking, controlled bleach cleaning, and moisture checks are the make-or-break steps.
  • How to choose a paver sealing company in Florida comes down to prep discipline and weather judgment.
  • The quote conversation is your interview; specific answers beat polished sales language every time.

Before hiring a paver sealing contractor, ask exactly how they handle cleaning, joint sand, moisture conditions, product selection, and warranty scope. These are the questions that reveal whether you are buying a real process or a fast cosmetic pass. Most paver sealing companies in Jacksonville sound similar at first, but the difference between a stable result and a failure within months is usually hidden in the prep steps they either explain clearly or avoid entirely.

The 10 Questions That Separate a Real Process From a Cheap Shortcut

1) Do you remove old joint sand before repacking, or add sand on top?

Why it matters: Old joint sand usually contains organic debris, ant activity, and contamination that keeps fresh sand from seating correctly. Adding new sand on top can look acceptable for a short period, then wash out quickly.

Strong answer: "We remove old sand, clean out the joints, and repack with ASTM C144 before sealing."

Weak answer: "We top it off where needed."

2) What type of sand do you use, and why?

Why it matters: Sand type affects joint stability, runoff resistance, and how evenly the final surface performs. HydroSeal uses ASTM C144 because it compacts and behaves predictably for this process.

Strong answer: "We use ASTM C144 and compact it correctly before sealer application."

Weak answer: "We use regular paver sand" with no further detail.

3) How do you handle efflorescence when you find it?

Why it matters: Sealing active efflorescence traps an active problem under film and usually brings the white bloom back. It has to be treated first, rinsed, and allowed to dry fully.

Strong answer: "We chemically treat efflorescence before sealing and do not seal active deposits."

Weak answer: "Pressure washing removes it" or "sealer will hide it."

4) What cleaning chemistry do you use?

Why it matters: Pressure alone does not fully address biological contamination. Controlled bleach use is typically what actually breaks down mold, algae, and organic growth in porous pavers.

Strong answer: "We use controlled bleach dilution based on contamination level, then rinse thoroughly."

Weak answer: "We just pressure wash."

5) What products do you use and why?

Why it matters: Product choice should match the surface and local climate, not just shelf availability. HydroSeal uses Trident-only products and can explain which product fits concrete pavers, pool decks, or travertine. You can review the product rationale here: Why We Use Trident Products Only.

Strong answer: Specific product name plus why it is selected for your surface.

Weak answer: "We use a premium sealer" with no product detail.

6) Are you certified or trained in the system you apply?

Why it matters: Certification does not replace experience, but it is a valuable quality signal when hiring a paver sealing contractor. It shows documented training in prep and application standards for that line.

Strong answer: "Yes, we are Trident Master Certified and can provide documentation."

Weak answer: "We've done this for years" with no product-line training details.

Learn more here: What Does Trident Master Certified Mean?.

7) How do you handle moisture, humidity, and weather windows?

Why it matters: In Florida, conditions control outcomes. The right company should explain surface moisture checks, humidity awareness, forecast review, and the flexibility to move between one-day and two-day scheduling when needed.

Strong answer: "We make the one-day vs two-day call based on condition readiness, not on a fixed promise."

Weak answer: "Every job is one day."

8) What is included in the price?

Why it matters: Price-per-square-foot is not useful if the scopes are different. A good quote breaks out prep, sand, treatment, sealer system, and exclusions.

Strong answer: A line-by-line scope with clear inclusions and exclusions.

Weak answer: "It covers everything" without written detail.

For local pricing context, read: How Much Does Paver Sealing Cost in Jacksonville.

9) How long should the results last in Jacksonville conditions?

Why it matters: Any answer that sounds permanent is unrealistic. A professional should give a practical range and explain what affects lifespan, including UV, irrigation, traffic, and maintenance habits.

Strong answer: A realistic maintenance window with clear expectations.

Weak answer: "You won't need service again for many years" without caveats.

10) What does your warranty actually cover?

Why it matters: Warranties vary widely. Ask what is covered, what voids it, and what normal wear is expected.

Strong answer: Specific covered failures and exclusions in writing.

Weak answer: "We stand behind our work" with no specifics.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • The company keeps redirecting to price instead of answering paver sealing quote questions.
  • They cannot name the exact product they plan to apply.
  • They do not mention old sand removal or moisture checks.
  • They guarantee one-day completion regardless of weather.
  • The warranty language is broad but not specific.

How to Use This Paver Sealing Contractor Checklist

You do not need to ask all ten questions in one call. Start with the most revealing four: old sand removal, efflorescence handling, cleaning chemistry, and weather/moisture scheduling. If those answers are clear and specific, continue with product, certification, pricing scope, and warranty details.

When two contractors look similar online, this checklist is how to choose a paver sealing company with confidence. Specificity is the signal; vagueness is the warning.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I ask before hiring a paver sealing company?

Start with process questions: do they remove old joint sand, what sand spec do they repack with, how do they treat efflorescence, what chemistry do they clean with, and how do they decide one-day versus two-day scheduling. Those answers tell you more than a price-per-square-foot number.

How many quotes should I get?

Two to three quotes is usually enough. Use the same checklist on every company so you can compare scope, prep depth, and product quality instead of just comparing totals.

Why are some paver sealing quotes so cheap?

Low quotes often skip labor-heavy steps like old sand removal, controlled chemistry cleaning, efflorescence treatment, and proper dry-time verification. Those skipped steps are what usually cause early haze, peeling, and sand loss.

Does certification matter?

Yes. Product-line certification is one useful quality signal because it shows the applicator has been trained on preparation and application requirements for that specific sealer system.

What is usually included in a professional paver sealing quote?

A professional quote should spell out cleaning chemistry, old joint sand removal, ASTM C144 repacking, any efflorescence treatment, sealer product name, number of coats, and warranty terms with clear exclusions.
