Driveway vs. Patio Sealing: What’s the Difference?

Driveways and patios may use similar pavers, but they do not age the same way. The way a surface is used should shape the prep, sealer choice, and maintenance plan. Homeowners often expect one sealing approach to work everywhere, but traffic, staining, shade, and water exposure usually call for different priorities.

Best Sealer for Pool Decks: Slip Safety and Durability

Pool decks ask more from a sealer than almost any other residential surface. They stay wet, collect sunscreen and organic film, and need to feel safe under bare feet. For Florida homeowners, the best pool deck sealer is not the one with the most dramatic shine. It is the one that fits the material, the moisture conditions, and the way the deck is actually used.

How Traffic Affects Paver Sealer Longevity

When homeowners ask how long sealer lasts, traffic is one of the first things to evaluate. Two surfaces sealed on the same day can age very differently depending on whether they handle vehicles, pool activity, furniture movement, or a simple backyard walkway. Traffic does not just wear the top visually. It also changes where cleaning, staining, and moisture problems show up first.
