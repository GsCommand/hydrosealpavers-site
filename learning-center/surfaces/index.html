Surface-Specific Paver & Travertine Guidance

Not every outdoor surface should be treated the same way. Driveways, patios, pool decks, walkways, and travertine each experience different traffic, moisture, finish expectations, and maintenance needs.

Florida Homeowner Context

Why this matters in Florida

Different paver surfaces in Florida experience different types of stress. Driveways deal with vehicle traffic and heat, while patios and pool decks are more affected by moisture, shade, and foot traffic. Each surface wears differently based on exposure and use.

These differences matter when it comes to maintenance and protection. A driveway may show early sealer breakdown, while a pool deck may develop algae or become slippery if not treated correctly.

Matching the process to the surface is what leads to better results. Proper cleaning, sanding, and sealing should always be adjusted based on how the surface is used and exposed.

FAQs

Common questions

Short answers for homeowners comparing options, timing, and next steps.

Should driveways use the same sealer as pool decks?

Not always. Driveways often need a solution suited for traffic and turning tires, while pool decks may need more attention to moisture exposure and finish feel.

Do walkways need sealing too?

Walkways can benefit from sealing, especially when they are exposed to weather, irrigation, staining, and washout around the joints.

Is travertine treated differently than concrete pavers?

Yes. Travertine typically requires a more careful product match and surface-specific prep because appearance and porosity differ.

Why does finish choice matter?

Gloss level, color enhancement, and traction expectations should match the use of the area rather than being chosen on looks alone.
