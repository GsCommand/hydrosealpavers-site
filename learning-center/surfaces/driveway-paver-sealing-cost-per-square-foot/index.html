Driveway Paver Sealing Cost Per Square Foot

Driveway paver sealing in Jacksonville starts at $1.50 per square foot. For most residential driveways, that typically lands between $600 and $1,200 before condition-related prep is factored in.

Learning Center HomeBack to Surfaces

TL;DR / Key Takeaways

  • $1.50/sq ft is the starting price for standard concrete paver driveways in good condition.
  • Larger driveways often cost less per square foot because fixed costs spread over more area.
  • Worn or neglected driveways cost more because condition drives prep scope.
  • Prep quality matters: skipping proper cleaning, sand work, or efflorescence treatment causes early failure.
  • Florida driveways typically need reseal maintenance every 2 to 3 years, not every 5+ years.

What $1.50 per square foot actually covers

At HydroSeal's base rate, a standard driveway job includes professional-grade bleach cleaning, removal of old joint sand, ASTM C144 joint sand repacking hydro-compacted into the joints, and Trident sealer applied at the correct film weight.

When weather and surface conditions cooperate, this can be completed in one day. If a quote is far below market, compare scope line by line because something in the prep or application sequence is often being skipped. Typical driveway cost ranges

Estimated ranges by size

  • Small (300 to 500 sq ft): $450 to $750
  • Medium (500 to 800 sq ft): $750 to $1,200
  • Large (800 to 1,200 sq ft): $1,200 to $1,800
  • Extra large (1,200+ sq ft): $1,800 to $2,400+

These ranges assume concrete pavers in reasonable condition. Travertine driveways are usually quoted separately because product compatibility and cleaning chemistry differ.

Why larger driveways often cost less per foot

Every project has fixed costs that do not scale with square footage: travel, setup, mixing product, and minimum time on site. A 400 sq ft driveway and a 900 sq ft driveway share many of the same setup costs, so larger projects can often receive a better per-foot rate.

This is common in communities with larger homes and expanded aprons, including Nocatee and newer parts of St. Johns County. Combined projects, like driveway plus rear patio, may also improve effective per-foot pricing.

Why badly worn driveways cost more

Heavy efflorescence

White mineral deposits require chemical treatment before sealing. That adds chemistry cost, labor, and dry time. Sealing over active efflorescence usually means it returns through the new film.

Severe joint sand loss

Driveways with mostly empty joints need more sand and more time to compact it correctly. A quick top-off is not enough for long-term stability.

Deep staining

Oil, rust, and embedded organic staining often need stronger chemistry and longer dwell time, sometimes across multiple cleaning passes.

Previously failed sealer

Peeling, flaking, or whitened old coatings may need correction before resealing. Some surfaces can be recoated, while others require stripping based on in-person inspection.

Driveway-specific wear factors in Florida

Northeast Florida driveways take a consistent combination of UV exposure, vehicle loading, irrigation overspray, and automotive fluids. These conditions can accelerate fade, joint movement, efflorescence recurrence, and stain retention.

Scheduling and process also matter. If your project timing depends on weather windows, review one-day vs. two-day paver sealing so expectations are realistic before booking.

Common mistakes when comparing driveway quotes

  • Comparing only price per foot without comparing scope details.
  • Assuming a lower quote includes the same prep and product standards.
  • Accepting firm pricing without seeing actual condition of the surface.

Square footage starts the estimate, but condition determines final scope. The most accurate quotes are based on in-person or detailed photo review.

Frequently asked questions

How much does driveway paver sealing cost per square foot?

Driveway paver sealing in Jacksonville starts at $1.50 per square foot for standard concrete pavers in good condition. Most residential driveways are 400 to 800 square feet, so many projects land between $600 and $1,200 before condition-related prep is added.

Why do larger driveways sometimes cost less per foot?

Every job has fixed costs like travel, setup, and minimum on-site time. Larger driveways spread those fixed costs across more square footage, so the per-foot rate can come down even when total price is higher.

What makes driveway sealing cost more?

Heavy efflorescence, major joint sand loss, deep oil or rust staining, and failed old sealer all add labor, chemistry, and dry-time requirements. Condition usually drives price changes more than square footage by itself.

How often should a driveway be resealed in Florida?

Every two to three years is a realistic maintenance range for most Florida driveways because UV, rain cycles, irrigation overspray, and vehicle traffic wear sealer faster than in milder climates.

Does travertine driveway sealing cost the same as concrete pavers?

Usually no. Travertine is quoted separately because cleaning chemistry, porosity behavior, and product requirements differ from standard concrete pavers.
