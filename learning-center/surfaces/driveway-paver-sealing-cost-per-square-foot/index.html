Driveway Paver Sealing Cost Per Square Foot

If you are comparing driveway sealing bids in Jacksonville and Northeast Florida, price per square foot is a starting point—not the full story.

TL;DR / Key Takeaways

  • Most standard driveway jobs start around $1.50/sq ft.
  • Large driveways can price lower per foot due to scale.
  • Prep requirements drive cost more than raw square footage.
  • Florida driveways usually need resealing every 2–3 years.
  • Travertine driveways are often priced separately from concrete pavers.

Typical driveway cost per square foot

For standard concrete pavers in good condition, many Jacksonville driveway jobs start near $1.50 per square foot. Smaller jobs may land higher per foot due to minimum service thresholds, while larger jobs may fall below baseline when prep is straightforward.

Example ranges

  • 400 sq ft driveway: often around $600 at baseline.
  • 800 sq ft driveway: often around $1,200 at baseline.
  • 1,200+ sq ft driveway: often priced with a scale adjustment after inspection.

Why some driveways cost more

Surface condition and contamination

Oil spots, rust staining, organic buildup, and older failing sealer can increase labor.

Joint stability and sand loss

Driveways with widespread washout need full sand correction before sealing for long-term stability.

Drainage and moisture patterns

Low spots, shade, and irrigation overspray can extend dry times and increase prep complexity.

Why larger driveways can cost less per foot

Setup, masking, equipment prep, and travel are fixed costs. When those are spread across a larger area, per-foot pricing often improves. This is common on larger lots in Nocatee and surrounding master-planned communities.

What a proper driveway sealing job includes

A quality process includes professional cleaning, joint sand correction, treatment for active mineral issues, and sealer application matched to the surface and weather window. For detailed local pricing context, see how much paver sealing costs in Jacksonville.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum cost for driveway paver sealing?

Most driveway sealing projects start around $900 for qualifying residential jobs. Final pricing is based on square footage, current condition, prep requirements, and access.

Why does one driveway cost more than another?

Pricing changes when a driveway needs more prep, deeper cleaning, stain treatment, heavier joint-sand correction, or more detailed edge work. Surface condition drives labor more than square footage alone.

Does stripping old sealer cost extra?

Yes. Stripping failed or incompatible old sealer is a separate restoration step and is not included in standard starting pricing. It adds labor, material, and time before resealing can begin.

How often should a driveway be resealed in Florida?

Most Florida driveways are resealed about every 2 to 3 years, depending on sun exposure, rain, traffic, and overall wear.
